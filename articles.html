CHEDWA
Isobar Walemba - June 10, 2024

After 6 dozens of months
I finally reached for his letter
One

he slid in my book
One I swore to never read

After 6 dozens of months
I finally heard his words
They echoed so loud and deep
Piercing the most secure walls
Forcing tears out my dry eyes

After 6 years of ignoring it
I finally read his letter
One he wrote his heart out
"You were never his...
You were just a bet..."
Words that came to life
A few weeks after he wrote them

After 6 good years
I'm here crying
If I had only opened it
My heart could have been saved
Now more then ever,
My heart is broken
As his coffin was lowered this morning

COULD HAVE, HAS NOT

My pen in my hand
My brain in my head
You in my arms
That's the proper composition

"Will you..." on my lips
"Yes..." on your lips
My palms on your hips
That's how things should go

Building a happy family together
As we create enough room
To raise a product of us, mating
That was the original script

A poem that will never be recited
As I met you late
Too bad, I'm not a cheater
And my wife is not dying soon
